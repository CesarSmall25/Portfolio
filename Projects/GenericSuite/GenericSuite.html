About Me

A quick summary of me and my accomplishments.

Who am I?

I’m a gameplay and UI programmer with several years of experience working in Unreal Engine using both C++ and Blueprints. My work focuses on building scalable, modular systems with a strong emphasis on maintainability, performance, and multiplayer correctness. I’ve designed and implemented a wide range of systems including inventory and economy, interaction and building mechanics, UI navigation, and online multiplayer workflows. I place a high value on clean architecture, data driven design, and creating solutions that can be reused across projects rather than tightly coupled to a single game.

I have professional experience working within team environments, including leading UI development, and I am also comfortable taking ownership of systems end to end when working independently. I approach development with a long term mindset, prioritizing clarity, extensibility, and polish throughout the production lifecycle.

I’m motivated by building reliable tools and gameplay systems that developers can trust and that players never have to think about. I value iteration, honest feedback, and continuous improvement, and I’m always looking to refine both my technical and creative approach to game development.

In My Spare Time

Right now I'm into electrical work. I've been tinkering lately with Arduinos and Raspberry Pis trying to learn more about that side of software engineering/IT work.

If I'm not tinkering with those I'm either playing games (as to be expected), swimming or spending time with friends/family or researching something to improve my development skillset.

My Aspirations

While I don't think I'll be the next Notch or anything, I am aiming to hone my skills as well as possible to become as good of a developer that I can and I hope to one day be able to proudly say that I'm either part of or have developed something with great meaning to a lot of people.

I simply want to be able to have a positive impact on the world through my work, no matter how big or small that impact may be.
